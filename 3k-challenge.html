What this series is

This series documents how I’m approaching the growth of a smaller account in real time. The framework stays the same: regimes, key levels, volatility posture / z-score, extrinsic divergence, ZWAP, and structure-based execution. What changes in a smaller account is the amount of cash-account flexibility available during the session.

Core idea

  • Same Foxchase framework.
  • Cash account structure is a feature, not a bug.
  • Goal is compounding with accountability.
  • Live commentary stays in Discord; this page serves as the archive.

Why exits may be conservative

  • Small accounts have less settled-fund flexibility.
  • Protecting realized gains matters for compounding.
  • The point is not to squeeze every last dollar from every move.
  • At larger balances, the framework stays the same. Flexibility just improves.
