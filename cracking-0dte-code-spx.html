Cracking the 0DTE Code: From Naïve Gamma to Live SPX Delta

By Adam Kimbrough — A Foxchase Trading Insight

8 min read · Just now

TL;DR

  • Static (“naïve”) gamma & delta maps built from yesterday’s OI are great for pre‑market terrain mapping but stale for intraday 0DTE timing because they ignore same‑day opening/closing flow.
  • Intraday CBOE Open–Close 10‑minute interval data shows actual customer positioning (Buy‑to‑Open vs Sell‑to‑Open) in Calls & Puts. Delta‑weighting via Trades/Quotes Greeks reveals real time dealer risk.
  • Directional Entry Rule: trade only on dominant Long Call or Long Put Buy‑to‑Open flow at/near your strike—fresh directional risk dealers must hedge.
  • Do not use Short Calls/Short Puts as primary triggers—they modulate or accelerate moves, but rarely initiate direction.
  • Price charts (ICT/SMC) are a “map” of potential liquidity; options flow is the “terrain.” Combine both.
  • Late‑day charm decay & end‑of‑day rebalancing can fuel outsized reversals or accelerations, ideal for “lotto” plays.

0. Map vs Territory: Charting, Flow, and the “Naïve” View of Information

The book Nexus by Yuval Harari describes a naive view of information as an attempt to represent reality; when wrong, it’s misinformation or disinformation. In markets, a price chart compresses order flow, inventory transfer, hedging, and structural constraints into a single trace.

“The naive view sees information as an attempt to represent reality… Misinformation is an honest mistake; disinformation is a deliberate lie. The naive view further believes that the solution to the problems caused by misinformation and disinformation is more information.”

ICT/SMC chart reading spots likely liquidity zones, but without flow you can’t see who’s trapped or must hedge. A tool like GEXStream gives a “flow ratio,” but lacks per‑strike breakdown. Intraday options flow is contract‑level, side‑tagged, open/close, strike, expiry. Delta‑weighting turns a 2D map into a high‑resolution terrain model of fuel for price moves.

When a market maker sells an option, they inherit delta. To hedge, they trade underlying; those hedge adjustments push markets.

Synthesis:

  • Stage entries at ICT levels.
  • Use LC/LP dominance for direction.
  • Use STO flows only as context.

1. Why 0DTE SPX Options Demand Better Flow Data

0DTE SPX volume has grown from single‑digits to over half of total SPX flow on peak days. Same‑day opening/closing risk dominates; yesterday’s OI is stale.

SPX options are European‑style, cash‑settled, large‑notional (~$100× index), trading nearly 24h (GTH + RTH). Each SPX contract is ~10× the notional of an at‑the‑money SPY option. Combined with no early‑assignment risk and 60/40 tax treatment, SPX is the go‑to for large same‑day S&P risk. Dealers warehouse this via SPX & ES futures, so SPX flow shows real hedge fuel. SPY flows fragment across smaller lots, dividends, and American‑style quirks. AM vs PM settlement clusters hedges into known windows.

2. Quick Greek Refresher for Intraday 0DTE Decisions

  • Delta = price sensitivity.
  • Gamma = rate of change of delta.
  • Charm = delta decay; explodes near expiry.

Long gamma hedges sell strength/buy weakness → dampening. Short gamma hedges chase → amplification. 0DTE accelerates these flows.

3. The “Naïve” Gamma / Delta Approach (Static OI Models)

3.1 What It Is

Tools like SpotGamma start with prior‑close OI, compute Greeks, assume dealer sign, and aggregate GEX & Dealer Delta.

3.2 Why Traders Use It

  • Morning terrain scan: big OI clusters.
  • Regime gauge: net gamma bias.
  • Key levels: gamma flips.

3.3 Structural Weaknesses for 0DTE

  • Stale OI until next morning.
  • Dealer sign heuristics can invert.
  • No open/close split.
  • Spread positions hidden.
  • No timing insight.

Conclusion: Static maps are context only—not live triggers.

4. Intraday CBOE Open–Close 10‑Minute Interval Data

4.1 What You Get

SPX/SPXW trades only on Cboe—no aggregation hassle. You get true 10‑min buckets of:

  • Call vs Put
  • Buy or Sell
  • Open or Close
  • Origin tag (customer/pro/MM)
  • Volume

4.2 Why It Matters for 0DTE

Dealers hedge same‑session flows. BTO vs STO per strike shows live inventory changes static OI misses.

4.3 The Four Core Buckets (Customer View → Dealer Implication)

Enter only on dominant LC or LP BTO at your level. Treat SC/SP as context (momentum, cap, reversion).

  • Heavy LC/LP: reversal/continuation via hedge flows.
  • Heavy SC/SP: accelerator or damper; size/trail only.
  • Gamma = targets; Delta = triggers.
  • Charm (~10–30 min left) → exit/lotto plays.
