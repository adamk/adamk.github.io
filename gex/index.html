Why We Built It

I never imagined Foxchase Trading would release a GEX tool.

There are already plenty of gamma exposure dashboards on the market, and I have never believed traders need another screen full of indicators, signals, and complicated features. GEX can be useful, but it is only one small piece of a much larger trading process.

For a long time, I personally paid nearly $40 per month for a GEX platform. My daily use was very simple: I opened the NDX 0DTE GEX chart, identified whether any GEX ramps or gamma pinning were present, and moved on with the trading day. I had little use for most of the additional indicators and extra features included with the subscription.

Eventually, one of the members of the Foxchase Trading community began developing his own solution. Together, we focused on the specific NDX gamma exposure view we actually use: GEX ramps, major concentrations, and potential gamma pinning areas.

How We Use GEX

We use GEX as a contextual tool, not a standalone trading system.

A large GEX concentration does not mean price must stop there. A gamma ramp does not guarantee continuation. A potential pin does not mean the market cannot move away from it.

At Foxchase Trading, GEX is used alongside market structure, key levels, regime context, volatility posture, and price action. It is one input in the decision process.

The goal is not to turn gamma exposure into a magic signal. The goal is to understand where important option-positioning pressure may exist during the NDX 0DTE session.
