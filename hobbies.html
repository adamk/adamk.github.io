My Hobbies

A few hobbies of mine include: Ham Radio, Hiking, and Mountain/Road Biking.



I received my first amateur radio license in October of 2014, just before my sophomore year of high school. Upon passing the Technician Class license test, I received my first callsign (KM4BHZ) along with permissions to a small frequency allocation. Then, in May of the following year, I passed the test for my General class license, which grants much more freedom and room to operate in the radio spectrum. During this time, I also applied for the callsign N4ADK as a vanity call, mainly because it includes my initials. As of February 13th, 2018, I tested for and passed the Extra Class license test, the 3rd and final ham license. I primarily use my university's club station for CW (morse code) on the HF bands. At home I like to use my portable QRP (low power) morse-code-only radio while hiking in the mountains. Recently, I had fun operating in the ARRL School Club Roundup contest and North American QSO Party (Collegiate Championship), earning NC State 4th place among universities in both contests. Projects I have enjoyed completing include: Radio Jove Telescope, tape measure yagi antenna, and a CubeSat project is in the near future. I also enjoy volunteering at local events that require ham communications as well as operating as a net control and spotter for Skywarn in Area 1 of Virginia. Beginning in 2018, I have taken on the position as a net control for the weekly Tuesday night VHF/UHF nets as W4ATC at the university club station.

Hiking in the outdoors has been an activity I have enjoyed throughout my life. I have extensively hiked the Appalachians in the western part of Virginia, and I have traveled to places such as Colorado, Maine, New Hampshire, Vermont, Nova Scotia, the Lake District (England), Ireland, and Scotland where I have enjoyed walking trails. I hope to finally begin backpacking soon.

Mountain biking and road biking have been something I have taken up seriously in the past few years. I started competitively racing cross country mountain bikes in high school during my junior year. Then, I bought my first road bike, a 1993 Specialized Allez, and fell in love with road biking as well. I continued racing mountain bikes on my high school team during my senior year as the team captain. Now, I am a member of NC State's cycling club where I do both road and mountain. If it's a nice day, odds are I'm out cycling.
